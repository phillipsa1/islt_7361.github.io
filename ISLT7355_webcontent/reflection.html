Personal Reflection


How I got here

I was first introduced to the thoery of instructional design during my undergraduate studies in human resource management class. There, I was asked to apply the theory of instructional design by creating multiple interactive PowerPoint Presentations for use in employee training programs. I created my first two courses on the topics of wrongful termination and employee training, and I fell in love! So, I began researching the industry. I took a few courses on LinkedIn, and Udemy, and joined some instructional design social media groups just to make sure ID was the right role for me. After researching the industry for several months. I found that instructional design combines everything I love in one neat package. So, I went ahead and enrolled in MUs' Learning Technologies and Design program. And the more I learn, the more I am certain I am going to love what I do.


Where I am

When I first began my program, I wasnâ€™t familiar with the ADDIE process and had no clue what each phase entailed. All I knew was that I wanted to work in L&D as an instructional designer. Yet, I had no idea how to complete front-end analysis, write objectives and little expereince creating courses. But all of that changed. I am halfway through my program, and so far, it has been amazing! Each one of the artifacts in my portfolio reflects my professional growth and development in my journey to become an instructional designer.


What I have Learned

This program has been very hands-on and I have gotten expereince working through each phase of the ADDIE lifecycle. I have learned how to conduct Front-End analysis. Identify discrepancies to close knowledge gaps through a variety of learning and training modalities. How to design and conduct formative and summative assessment/evaluation. I have gained a solid understanding of adult learning thoery. Now, I feel confident in my abilities to write objectives and use Backwards Design to ensure my objectives, assessments, and learning strategies are well aligned, and so much more! With each class I am slowly, but surely becoming an informed instructional designer. Equipped with the basic tools, knowledge and know-how needed to create professional courses.


Where I am going

After graduation I plan to use my new skills to begin a career in instructional design building quality training instructions that can be delivered both in-person or online. I want to work on a design team for a large corporation as their in-house training specialist, or as an instructional design consultant and work under contract for a variety of businesses to help them achieve their training goals and objectives. I know a career in instructional design will keep me stimulated intellectually and challenged professionally as it will allow me to triangulate where problems are coming from, and use my skills to create meaningful training programs that help to cut organizational inefficiencies while simultaneously making a positive impact for thousands of trainees lives. While I am proud of the work accomplished so far, I am excited about my future and the opportunities that lie ahead.
